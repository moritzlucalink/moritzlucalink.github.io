hi :)
i love working on problems - every problem, no matter how niche, reveals a unique landscape when explored deeply. discovering this landscape and uncovering its hidden patterns through structured thinking excites me. trained over years by solving mathematical problems, i have developed expertise in breaking down complexity, identifying key questions, and crafting creative solutions that respect constraints and meet stakeholders needs. what drives me most is applying this approach to real-world challenges, where problem-solving can create tangible impact and contribute to a sustainable, thriving future. i enjoy collaborating in diverse teams, where different perspectives and requirements come together to shape well-rounded, effective solutions. to ensure my approaches are grounded on solid reasoning, i leverage data-driven methods to derive evidence-based solutions that are both practical and impactful.


my background

i am a mathematician holding a bachelor's degree in mathematics, with a minor in philosophy, from FAU, a master's degree in mathematics as well as a doctoral degree in mathematics from university of konstanz. from january 2021 to december 2024, i worked as a reasearch associate in the numerical optimization research group at the university of konstanz, under the supervision of prof dr stefan volkwein. my position was funded by the transferplatform, an initiave at the university of konstanz focused on developing multi-objective energy supply network models that incorporate homeowner acceptance of retrofit measures.


my research contributions

my role within the transferplatform initiative was diverse. i first collaborated with the research group sociology and survey research to identify suitable modeling approaches for homeowner acceptance. next, i integrated maximizing homeowner acceptance of retrofit measures as an additional objective function into an existing energy supply network model, formulating a multi-objective optimization problem. beyond modeling, i developed novel algorithms capable of solving multi-objective mixed-integer nonlinear optimization problems. these algorithms were analyzed both theoretically (proving convergence and finiteness) and computationally. for practical implementation, i built an optimization software in python using the modeling language pyomo and the solvers scip and gurobi. as a strong advocate for open-source development, i actively share my work on my github page, ensuring my code is accessible to everybody.


what i currently do

i handed in my doctoral thesis in december 2024, defended in june 2025, and submitted the final version in july 2025. in the meantime, i worked on the revisions of two academic papers as well as on the development of a bot intended to support in-game sports betting on the basis of reinforcement learning. additionally, i am deeply engaged in reading about how recent AI advancements are shaping our society, exploring both their potential and the challenges they bring.

if you are interested in my work or would like to collaborate, feel free to reach out!
